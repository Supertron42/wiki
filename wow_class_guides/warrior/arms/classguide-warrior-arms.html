WoW Classic Anniversary TBC - Arms Warrior DPS Guide

Your fast track to a raid-ready Arms Warrior in WoW Classic Anniversary (TBC). Pre-raid BiS, per-phase raid upgrades, talents, gems, enchants, consumables, and the DPS rotation, with every pick ranked by consensus across the major Arms guides, sources verified against live loot tables, and notes checked against live item stats. Hover any item, boss, or ability for full Wowhead tooltips.

Overview

Arms Warriors are TBC's bursty 2H melee DPS, built around Mortal Strike and, more importantly, Blood Frenzy, the talent that makes your bleeds buff all physical damage the target takes by 4%, a raid-wide DPS gain that is the entire reason to bring an Arms Warrior. This guide ranks gear per slot (consensus of wowtbc.gg, Icy Veins, and Warcraft Tavern), verifies every source against the in-game loot tables, and grounds every note in real item stats, with hover tooltips on everything.

1. Hit the caps first. 9% melee hit (142 Hit Rating) to clear the white-swing miss chance against bosses, and 6.5% expertise (~26 expertise, 103 Expertise Rating) to remove boss dodges. These two caps are your biggest DPS gains, sort them before stacking anything else.

2. Then follow the stat priority: Hit (to 9%) > Expertise (to 6.5%) > Critical Strike > Armor Penetration > Strength / Attack Power > Agility > Haste.

Rotation

Arms is a priority system on a slow 2H weapon: cast the highest available ability, weave Slam between swings, and dump spare Rage into Heroic Strike. Built from the Icy Veins and Warcraft Tavern Arms guides.

The whole rotation in one line: keep Rend up for Blood Frenzy, then Mortal Strike on cooldown > Whirlwind on cooldown > Slam as filler > Heroic Strike as the Rage dump.

Single-Target Priority

Top to bottom, use the first ability that is off cooldown and affordable:

  1. Rend kept on the target. It is a weak hit on its own, but its bleed (with Deep Wounds from your crits) keeps Blood Frenzy active, buffing the whole raid's physical damage on that target.
  2. Mortal Strike on cooldown. Your hardest-hitting strike and it applies Mortal Wounds.
  3. Whirlwind on cooldown. Strong instant damage; pairs with Mortal Strike off the same Rage pool.
  4. Slam as filler between swings. With Improved Slam its cast fits inside your 2H swing timer; cast it only when Mortal Strike and Whirlwind are both on cooldown and you are not about to auto-attack.
  5. Overpower when it procs (target dodges), if you are in Battle Stance; it cannot be dodged/blocked and crits hard with Impale.
  6. Heroic Strike as your Rage dump. Queue it on your next swing whenever Rage is plentiful; it does not cost a global.

Execute Phase (sub-20%)

  • Below 20% boss health, Execute becomes your top priority and replaces Slam/Heroic Strike as your Rage sink.
  • Still use Mortal Strike and Whirlwind on cooldown, and keep Rend/Blood Frenzy up, then pour everything else into Execute.
  • Line up Death Wish and trinkets for the execute window when possible for a big finish.

Multi-Target / AoE

  • Sweeping Strikes then Whirlwind and Cleave to spread damage to extra targets.
  • Thunder Clap and Rend on multiple targets for bleed/AoE pressure.
  • On true AoE packs, Warriors are filler DPS; focus Sweeping Strikes + Whirlwind on cooldown and let the AoE classes lead.

Rage Management

  • On a slow 2H, Rage comes in chunks; do not empty the bar on Heroic Strike if it would delay Mortal Strike or Whirlwind.
  • Queue Heroic Strike only when Rage is plentiful; skip it when banking for your strikes.
  • Open with Bloodrage so you start with a Rage pool, and use Berserker Rage to refill in long fights.

Keep These Up

  • Battle Shout (talented with Commanding Presence) for the whole raid's attack power.
  • Rend / Deep Wounds on the boss so Blood Frenzy stays active for the melee group.
  • Five stacks of Sunder Armor on the boss (raid-shared) so your physical hits land harder.

Talents

The raid build is deep Arms with a Fury dip: about 30+ points in Arms for Mortal Strike, Impale, Two-Handed Weapon Specialization, your weapon specialization, Improved Slam, and the payoff talent Blood Frenzy; the rest into Fury for Death Wish, Cruelty, Commanding Presence, and Improved Berserker Rage. For the exact point-by-point spread and filler choices, see the Icy Veins talents page linked below.

Key talents and why:

  • Blood Frenzy (Arms): bleeds make the target take +4% physical damage from everyone. The signature raid-DPS contribution.
  • Mortal Strike (Arms): your core instant strike and the spec's namesake.
  • Impale (Arms): +20% critical damage on Mortal Strike, Slam, Overpower, and Execute.
  • Improved Slam (Arms): cuts Slam's cast so it fits inside the 2H swing timer, a real DPS gain.
  • Weapon Specialization (Arms): 5 points in Poleaxe, Sword, or Mace to match your equipped weapon.
  • Death Wish (Fury): +20% damage cooldown.
  • Commanding Presence (Fury): stronger Battle Shout for the whole raid.

Gemming Priority

Questing Strategy

Quests reward gear, gold, rep, and XP all at once, but only if you batch them. The pickup-everything-first workflow saves hours of backtracking and turns every mob kill into 3-4 simultaneous progress bars (rep + quest + farm + Marks for Aldor turn-ins).

Universal rule: When you arrive at a quest hub or new zone, walk every NPC with a yellow ! before doing anything else. Then leave town with a full quest log (25 quests) and a plan that completes 4-6 quests per loop. Never kill or gather "just to grind", there's almost always a quest objective for that mob/node.

Link convention: Entries shown as "[Item Name] quest" use the quest ID from the source BiS guides. Hover the link to see the actual quest title, NPC, and location. Names displayed literally (like Hero of the Mag'har or Teron Gorefiend, I am...) are confirmed; generic "[Item] quest" labels are used where the source guide didn't spell out the quest title.

Export to AtlasLoot

Send every gear pick in this guide straight into AtlasLoot so the recommended items show up as favourites (gold stars) on the in-game loot tables. Requires the AtlasLoot Wishlist addon (v1.3+).

Just want one slot or one phase? Every gear table has its own Export to AtlasLoot button that lets you grab that single slot or that whole phase. Use the export below for the entire guide at once.

  1. Pick which phases to include (all on by default), then click Copy Export String.
  2. In game, type /alimport (or click Import on the AtlasLoot Wishlist tab).
  3. Name the list (e.g. "Arms Warrior"), paste with Ctrl+V, and click Import.
  4. Browse any raid or dungeon in AtlasLoot: this guide's items are now favourited, and each slot's BiS pick is flagged as the main item.
