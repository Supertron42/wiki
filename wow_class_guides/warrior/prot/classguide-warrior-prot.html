WoW Classic Anniversary TBC - Protection Warrior Tank Guide

Your fast track to a raid-ready Protection Warrior in WoW Classic Anniversary (TBC). Pre-raid BiS, per-phase raid upgrades, gems, enchants, consumables, and the max-threat rotation, with every pick ranked by consensus across the major tank guides and checked against live item stats. Hover any item, boss, or ability for full Wowhead tooltips.

Overview

Protection Warriors are TBC's premier main tank: the strongest single-target threat, the deepest cooldown kit (Shield Wall, Last Stand, Shield Block), and the best physical mitigation in the game. Gear it right and you main-tank everything from Karazhan to Sunwell. Two numbers drive the whole build, sort these first:

1. Hit the Defense cap. 490 Defense (you start with 350 at level 70, so you need +140 from gear, talents, and enchants) makes you uncrittable by raid bosses. Priority #1 before stacking anything else.

2. Then follow the stat priority: Defense (to cap) > Stamina > Dodge/Parry/Block > Hit/Expertise (threat) > Block Value (trash/heroics).

Rotation & Threat

Tank threat is a priority system, not a fixed sequence: cast the highest item on the list that is available and affordable, then auto-attack. Maximising threat (and your own damage) comes down to never wasting a global cooldown and dumping spare Rage into Heroic Strike. Built from the Icy Veins and Warcraft Tavern Protection rotation guides.

The whole rotation in one line: Shield Block (defense) > Shield Slam > Revenge > Devastate filler, and spend leftover Rage on Heroic Strike. On a 6 sec cooldown, Shield Slam lands every 4th global, giving the classic loop Shield Slam > Revenge > Devastate > Devastate.

Single-Target Threat Priority

Top to bottom, use the first ability that is off cooldown and affordable:

  1. Shield Block on cooldown when taking heavy damage. It grants crush immunity while active, so keep it up on bosses. You may skip it if Rage-starved and you need threat, but that risks a crushing blow.
  2. Shield Slam on cooldown. Your single biggest threat ability, and it ignores armor.
  3. Revenge whenever it lights up (after a block, dodge or parry). Highest threat per Rage in the kit.
  4. Devastate as filler. It applies and refreshes Sunder Armor, so spam it at the start of a pull to stack the debuff to 5 quickly, then keep it rolling.
  5. Heroic Strike as your Rage dump. Ideally every auto-attack is queued as a Heroic Strike when Rage allows; it is "on next swing" and does not cost a global.

Heroic Strike vs Devastate: once Sunder Armor (or Expose Armor) is at full stacks and you have Rage to spare, Heroic Strike out-threats another Devastate. Devastate is your filler only when you cannot afford to dump Rage.

Pull & Opener

  1. Use Bloodrage between pulls to enter combat with a Rage pool. If you pull with a ranged weapon, use it just after combat starts so it spreads a little threat onto everything.
  2. If you are below ~10 Rage and the pack does not fear, drop to Berserker Stance and use Berserker Rage (more Rage from damage taken), then return to Defensive Stance.
  3. Open with a ranged shot pull, or Charge from Battle Stance into the pack. Land Thunder Clap for multi-mob packs or Shield Slam for one to two targets, then settle into Defensive Stance and run the priority above.

Multi-Target / AoE Threat

This is where Prot is weakest. Think of it as "juggling plates": track your threat lead on every mob and be ready to stun or taunt the one about to break loose.

  • 2 to 3 mobs: run the single-target priority and rotate your target every global so threat builds evenly. Tab through them rather than tunnelling one.
  • Thunder Clap on cooldown: solid AoE threat plus a 20% attack-speed slow (talented).
  • Cleave replaces Heroic Strike as your Rage dump on packs; it hits a second target.
  • Demoralizing Shout hits everything for threat and cuts enemy attack power (mitigation too).
  • Whirlwind (from Berserker Stance) adds burst AoE damage/threat on big pulls; use situationally since it leaves Defensive Stance.
  • Recover a loose mob with Taunt, Concussion Blow (stun + threat), or Challenging Shout for a whole-pack emergency.

Rage Management

  • Learn the Rage cost of each ability and keep your Rage bar where you can see it.
  • Reserve enough Rage to re-cast Shield Block the moment it falls off; do not spend yourself empty on Heroic Strike.
  • Think a couple of globals ahead: if Shield Block drops in 2 sec, bank the Rage for it now.
  • When Rage is flowing, dump everything into Heroic Strike (or Cleave on packs); idle Rage is wasted threat.
  • Use Bloodrage between pulls so you never start a fight empty.

Keep These Up

  • Whichever of Battle Shout or Commanding Shout you are assigned, for the whole fight.
  • Demoralizing Shout for the enemy attack-power reduction.
  • Thunder Clap for the attack-speed slow, on cooldown.
  • Five stacks of Sunder Armor (via Devastate) on the boss for the raid.

Aldor vs Scryers

Both factions work fine for a Prot Warrior, the shoulder enchant difference is negligible (0.02% avoidance), so the meaningful trade-off is the Phase 5 SSO necklace proc and the side perks. Pick whichever fits your account/server economy and stick with it.

Phase 1 - Karazhan / Gruul's Lair / Magtheridon's Lair

Phase 1 is the T4 Warbringer Armor era plus off-set drops from Karazhan, Gruul and Magtheridon. The rankings below are the consensus across the major tank guides. Where the survival pick and the threat pick genuinely split, both are listed: take the survival item until you are crit-immune (490 Defense), then move to the threat item. The standout drop is the Aldori Legacy Defender shield from Gruul, which stays BiS for many phases.

T4 Set Bonuses (Warbringer Armor): 2pc = chance on parry to gain Blade Turning (absorbs 200 damage for 15 sec). 4pc = your Revenge makes your next damaging ability deal 10% more damage. The bonuses are weak, so several tier pieces are beaten by off-set, PvP or world-boss items. Tokens (Fallen Defender): Helm Prince Malchezaar, Gloves The Curator, Shoulders High King Maulgar, Legs Gruul, Chest Magtheridon.

Two warrior T4 sets share the same tokens: Warbringer Battlegear (the -plates/-helm/-gauntlets threat pieces) and Warbringer Armor (the -guards survival pieces). Once you are crit-immune the threat pieces win at most slots, so the threat version is the BiS and the survival version is the avoidance alternative.

Gems

Gemming as Prot Warrior is straightforward: stack pure Stamina. Stamina gems carry 50% more stat budget than other primary stats, and Stamina is further boosted 5% by Vitality. Ignore most socket bonuses unless they give +3 Stamina or more. Sourced from the Wowhead Protection Warrior Tank Gems & Enchants Guide.

Budget gem: If your server's economy makes Solid Star of Elune (+12 Sta) too expensive, the rare-quality Solid Azure Moonstone (+9 Sta) is a perfectly acceptable pre-raid substitute.

Meta Gem: When to Switch

Start the expansion in Powerful Earthstorm Diamond, it's cheap, requires 3 blue gems (which you have plenty of), and the +18 Stamina is your best stat. Switch to Relentless Earthstorm Diamond later when:

  • Higher item levels and epic gems give you enough Stamina to feel safe
  • Your PvP gear and jewelry stack Crit/Agi making the +3% crit damage worthwhile
  • Hard-hitting bosses spike your rage generation, increasing Heroic Strike usage
  • You've picked up Impale for the multiplicative crit-damage scaling

Cost of Relentless: requires 2 yellow + 2 red gems active to qualify. That replaces 4 pure-stamina blues with orange/green/purple gems and is a noticeable EHP loss. Only switch when you're comfortable with your survival numbers.

Glyph of the Defender (Keepers of Time - Revered) is the BiS tank head enchant. Skip Glyph of Ferocity, that's a Fury enchant.

Weapon (Phase Outlook)

Mongoose dominates Phase 1. When Enchant Weapon - Executioner unlocks (Phase 3), consider running Executioner on a threat-focused mainhand and Mongoose on a survival/swap weapon.

Questing Strategy

Quests reward gear, gold, rep, and XP all at once, but only if you batch them. The pickup-everything-first workflow saves hours of backtracking and turns every mob kill into 3-4 simultaneous progress bars (rep + quest + farm + Marks for Aldor turn-ins).

Universal rule: When you arrive at a quest hub or new zone, walk every NPC with a yellow ! before doing anything else. Then leave town with a full quest log (25 quests) and a plan that completes 4-6 quests per loop. Never kill or gather "just to grind", there's almost always a quest objective for that mob/node.

Link convention: Entries shown as "[Item Name] quest" use the quest ID from the Wowhead Prot Warrior Pre-Raid BiS guide. Hover the link to see the actual quest title, NPC, and location. Names displayed literally (like Hero of the Mag'har or Teron Gorefiend, I am...) are confirmed; generic "[Item] quest" labels are used where the source guide didn't spell out the quest title.
